🧠 AI Prompting Techniques Reference 7 Techniques
▼

Click on any technique to see detailed explanation and examples

R-C-T-F Framework
Structure prompts with Role, Context, Task, and Format for consistent, high-quality outputs.
Use for: Any prompt where you need specific, structured output
Zero-Shot Prompting
Ask AI to perform a task with no examples - just clear instructions.
Use for: Simple, well-defined tasks where AI's default knowledge is enough
Few-Shot Prompting
Provide 2-4 examples before your request so AI learns the pattern you want.
Use for: Consistent formatting, specific style, custom transformations
Chain of Thought (CoT)
Ask AI to "think step by step" before giving the final answer.
Use for: Complex problems, debugging, logic-heavy tasks
Role-Based Prompting
Assign AI a specific expert persona before the task.
Use for: Domain-specific expertise, different perspectives
Constraint-Based Prompting
Set explicit boundaries on what AI can/cannot do, include or exclude.
Use for: Focused answers, specific limits, excluding solutions
Iterative Refinement
Build on AI's response with follow-up prompts to improve output.
Use for: Complex documents, when first output needs improvement
